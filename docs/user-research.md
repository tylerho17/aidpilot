# AidPilot User Research Doc

## Purpose

This document tracks what students actually need from AidPilot. The goal is to make sure AidPilot is built around real student confusion, not random features.

## 1. Research goal

Understand how students currently manage financial aid, deadlines, documents, school portals, and scholarships. Identify where AidPilot can reduce confusion, prevent missed aid, and help students know what to do next.

## 2. Target users

- First-generation college students
- Low-income students
- California public university students
- Community college transfer students
- Students receiving Pell Grant, Cal Grant, work-study, or loans
- Students who have missed or almost missed a financial aid deadline
- Students confused by FAFSA, school aid portals, or scholarship applications
- Students applying to scholarships while already in college

## 3. Current hypotheses

1. Students do not know if their financial aid is safe until something goes wrong.

2. Students struggle more with sequencing than with individual forms.

3. Students treat scholarships as random, noisy, and overwhelming.

4. Students want reminders, but only if the reminders are specific and trustworthy.

5. Students are nervous about sharing sensitive financial information.

6. Students want to know what to do next, not read long financial aid explanations.

7. Aid offices are overloaded because students ask repetitive questions.

8. Weekly check-ins may be more useful than one-time FAFSA guidance.

9. Students trust school-specific and deadline-specific information more than generic advice.

10. Students need confidence, not just information.

## 4. Interview script

I'm building AidPilot, a tool that helps students protect their financial aid, track deadlines, and find scholarships weekly. I'm not trying to sell you anything. I just want to understand if this would actually help.

**Questions:**

1. How do you currently keep track of financial aid deadlines?

2. Have you ever worried that your aid might be delayed, reduced, or missing?

3. What is the most confusing part of FAFSA or your school aid portal?

4. Do you know what documents your school needs from you right now?

5. Have you ever missed or almost missed a financial aid task?

6. How do you currently find scholarships?

7. What makes you not apply to scholarships?

8. Would a weekly aid check-in be useful?

9. What would make you trust or not trust a tool like AidPilot?

10. What would you want AidPilot to do first?

11. Would you want email reminders, dashboard reminders, or both?

12. Would you be comfortable creating an account?

13. What information would you never want to share with a tool like this?

14. What would make this feel like a real product instead of another school portal?

15. If AidPilot only did one thing well, what should that be?

## 6. Early patterns

- Students want to know if they are okay.
- Students want specific next steps.
- Students do not want to upload sensitive documents until trust is earned.
- Scholarship discovery feels noisy.
- Aid portal language feels institutional and confusing.
- Students often do not know the difference between FAFSA, state aid, school aid, grants, work-study, and loans.
- Students need help with deadlines and documents more than broad education content.
- A weekly check-in could become the habit loop.
- Trust and safety copy needs to be visible.
- The product should feel calm, not bureaucratic.

## 7. Product decisions from research

- Keep the main dashboard focused on aid safety.
- Show the next 3 urgent tasks, not every task at once.
- Keep document tracker status-only for now.
- Do not collect tax documents, SSNs, or FAFSA login credentials.
- Make scholarship matches explain why they fit.
- Use weekly check-ins as the core product habit.
- Use plain English instead of financial aid jargon.
- Separate demo data from real saved user data.
- Build email reports only after confirming students want recurring reminders.
- Treat school-specific workflows as a future expansion.

## 8. Open questions

- Will students come back weekly?
- Should AidPilot send email reports?
- Which scholarships are worth surfacing first?
- Should AidPilot support school-specific portals?
- How much personalization is needed before the tool feels useful?
- Do students trust a startup with financial aid guidance?
- What is the right level of automation?
- Should the first product focus on FAFSA protection or scholarship discovery?
- Should AidPilot start student-first or school-office-first?
- What information should never be stored?
